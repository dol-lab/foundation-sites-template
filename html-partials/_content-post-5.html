A rant on foundation variables

Foundation's color system is a straitjacket. Change $primary-color or a palette token and it insists on auto-cooking tints, shades, and states with scale-color(), mix(), and friends—so one tweak cascades into off-brand buttons, weird hovers, and “smart” contrast that isn't. Swap in CSS variables and Sass screams, because those color helpers expect literals, not var().

And then you're doing node-module archaeology: digging through foundation-sites/scss/settings/_settings.scss, palettes, and utilities just to find which component recalculates your colors next. You copy half the settings file, override $foundation-palette, whack a dozen “helpful” computations, and pray nothing else re-derives a hover from your hover. It's not theming; it's whack-a-mole with color math.
